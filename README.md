# Synthesis of ZnO Nanoparticles using Sol-Gel Method

## Aim
To synthesize zinc oxide (ZnO) nanoparticles using the sol-gel method.

---

## Materials Required

### Chemicals
- **Precursor:** Zinc nitrate (Zn(NO₃)₂)
- **Solvent:** Distilled water  
- **Base:** Sodium hydroxide (NaOH)  
- **Reducing Agent:** Calcium carbonate (CaCO₃)  
- **Surfactant:** Sodium dodecyl sulphate (SDS)  

### Apparatus
- Beaker  
- Conical flask  
- Measuring cylinder  
- Magnetic stirrer with hot plate  
- Burette  
- Thermometer  
- pH paper / pH meter  

---

## Procedure

1. **Preparation of Zinc Nitrate Solution**  
   Dissolve zinc nitrate (Zn(NO₃)₂) in distilled water and stir well using a magnetic stirrer.

2. **Addition of Surfactant**  
   Add sodium dodecyl sulphate (SDS) to the solution and continue stirring.

3. **Preparation of Calcium Carbonate Suspension**  
   Mix calcium carbonate (CaCO₃) with distilled water in a separate beaker.

4. **Mixing of Solutions**  
   Slowly add the calcium carbonate suspension into the zinc nitrate solution with continuous stirring.

5. **Formation of Precipitate**  
   Maintain temperature at 60–70°C while stirring until a white precipitate forms.

6. **Filtration and Washing**  
   Filter the precipitate and wash it with distilled water to remove impurities.

7. **Drying**  
   Dry the precipitate at room temperature or in a hot air oven.

8. **Calcination**  
   Heat the dried powder in a furnace at 400–600°C to obtain ZnO nanoparticles.

---

## Result
ZnO nanoparticles were successfully synthesized using the sol-gel method.
